"Lô tô" refers to a type of lottery or bingo-style game that is popular in Vietnam. The game involves a set of numbered tiles or cards, and players select a combination of numbers. The winning numbers are then drawn randomly, and players check if their chosen numbers match the drawn ones. If a player successfully matches a specified pattern or sequence, they win the game and receive a prize.

"Lô tô" is commonly played as a form of entertainment at social gatherings, festivals, and during Tet, the Lunar New Year celebration. It is a game that combines luck and chance, making it accessible and enjoyable for a wide range of participants.

The term "lô tô" can also refer to a type of bingo card used in the game. These cards typically have a grid of numbers, and players mark the numbers as they are called out during the game. The goal is to achieve a specific pattern on the card before other players to win a prize.

Pháo Hoa (fireworks)


Fireworks are a significant and popular element of Tet, the Lunar New Year celebration in Vietnam. The use of fireworks during Tet is a long-standing tradition and a symbol of the festive and celebratory atmosphere that accompanies the holiday.


Fireworks are believed to ward off evil spirits and bring good luck for the coming year. The loud noises and bright colors are thought to scare away negative forces and create a joyful and auspicious beginning to the new lunar year. In major cities and towns across Vietnam, local governments often organize large-scale fireworks displays to mark the arrival of Tet. These displays are usually held in public spaces, providing a communal experience for residents and visitors.


The most significant fireworks displays typically occur at midnight on New Year's Eve, signaling the transition from the old year to the new one. This moment is accompanied by various customs and traditions and including family gatherings. Traditionally, firecrackers were a common feature of Tet celebrations. However, due to safety concerns and regulations in some urban areas, the use of firecrackers has been restricted or banned. Fireworks displays have become a safer alternative.

Lì Xì


"Lì xì" is a Vietnamese tradition of giving and receiving red envelopes or red packets during Tet, the Lunar New Year. The red envelopes, known as "bao lì xì" or simply "lì xì," are typically filled with money and symbolize good luck, prosperity, and blessings for the recipient.


The practice of giving "lì xì" is a gesture of goodwill and blessings. It is common for married couples or older family members to give red envelopes to younger family members, especially children. Employers may also give "lì xì" to employees as a token of appreciation. When receiving a red envelope, it is customary to express gratitude and well-wishes to the giver. The act of giving and receiving "lì xì" is accompanied by words of blessing for good health, prosperity, and happiness in the new year.


Children, in particular, look forward to receiving "lì xì" during Tet. The act of collecting red envelopes becomes an exciting and joyful part of the holiday for them.

Múa Lân


"Múa lân" refers to the Lion Dance, a traditional Vietnamese and Chinese performance that is often seen during festive occasions, including Tet (Lunar New Year). The Lion Dance is a symbolic and lively performance that involves dancers wearing a lion costume to bring good luck, fortune, and prosperity to the community.


The Lion Dance is believed to ward off evil spirits and bring good luck and positive energy. The lion is considered a symbol of strength, courage, and protection. Its costume is typically colorful and adorned with various decorations. It consists of a lion's head and a cloth body. The head is often animated with movable eyes, mouth, and ears. The body is manipulated by two dancers who are hidden inside.


The Lion Dance is accompanied by traditional music, including drums, cymbals, and gongs. The rhythmic beats and lively melodies add to the festive atmosphere and enhance the performance. It involves intricate and acrobatic movements. The lion may perform various gestures, such as stretching, leaping, and mimicking actions like eating or playing with a ball. These movements symbolize the lion's vitality and joy.
